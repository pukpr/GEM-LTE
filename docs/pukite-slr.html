Sea-level rise (SLR) at Warnemünde fitted via the GEM-LTE pipeline: 31-constituent tidal sum → biannual impulse → IIR integration → LTE manifold → MLR. Training window defaults to 1940–1970. The nonlinear tidal manifold — reproduced from first principles using YEAR_LEN = 365.24725 d — is the centrepiece.

The LTE pipeline: (1) 31 tidal constituents summed as cos-phase waves with YEAR_LEN = 365.24725 d; (2) biannual impulse (month 10 × delA, month 4 × asym) multiplied pointwise; (3) bidirectional IIR integrator seeded at IDATE = 1920.9; (4) MLR with regressors [1, m, sin/cos(2πk⋅m)] for k ∈ {23.24, 9.47, 75.73, 227.20}. Annual/semi cycles are subtracted before regression and restored in the fit.

      

      

    

    

      Data notes
      
Monthly mean sea level at Warnemünde 2, Germany (54.17°N, 12.10°E), 1855.25–2024.17 (2028 values, mm). Tidal constituents from experiments/Feb2026/11/lt.exe.p (lpap array, 31 entries). IIR parameters: lag = mp = −7.718×10−5, init = −1.405×10−4. Wave numbers k from ltep = [23.24, 9.47] extended by harm = [8, 24] multiplied onto ltep[1]. YEAR_LEN = 365.24725 d = 365.2422484 + 0.005 (YEAR env from lt.exe.resp). Reproducing the tidal manifold without free parameters validates the GEM-LTE pipeline.
